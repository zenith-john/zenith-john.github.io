Categorification of Donaldson-Thomas Theory

[2021-12-31 Fri 15:26] The Calabi-Yau 3-fold case is defined by algebraic methods of [BF1997] in [Thomas2000]. Since then a lot of study in DT theory emerges. Categorification of Donaldson-Thomas theory is one of important problems in Donaldson-Thomas theory. The physicists view Donaldson-Thomas invariants as dimension of BPS states. Mathematically, the problem is raised in [KS2008] and [JS2012] in somehow different manner.

The approach in [KS2008] is to defined Donaldson-Thomas invariants in motivic rings. By taking the Euler characteristic, the motivic invariants descents to numerical Donaldson-Thomas invariants. Again in confiold case [MMN+2012], the motivic refinement matches refined topological vertex defined in [DG2009] as original DT theory matches topological vertex. In [BBS2013], the result of dimension zero numerical DT invariants was recovered.

The approach in [JS2012] is to define some perverse sheaves and realized the numerical ones as Euler characteristics again. This approach is later generalized to Saito’s mixed hodge module theory as mentioned in [BBS2013]. These perverse sheaves techniques together with critical loci was used to defined Gopakumar-Vafa invariants in [MT2018].

More recently, Toda suggest some new approaches in categorification based on derived algebraic geometry discussed in next section. In a seris of paper by [Toda2021b, Toda2021a, Toda2021], he define and study some version of derived category of moduli spaces relating to Donaldson-Thomas theory. The numerical invariants are Euler characteristic of periodic cyclic homology of the category.

Derived algebraic geometry and Donaldson-Thomas theory for 4-folds

The idea of Donaldson-Thomas theory originated in [DT1998], where Donaldson and Thomas suggested some gauge-theoretic analog of 3 dimensional Chern-Simons theory and 4 dimensional Donaldson theory to higher dimensional manifolds, for example Calabi-Yau 3-fold, \(G_{2}\) manifold and \(Spin(7)\) manifold (also higher dimesional CY varieties). For a long time, only CY3 case is established in [Thomas2000] and widely studied and other 2 cases is unknown.

By the injection \(SU(4) \to Spin(7)\), the CY 4-fold can be viewed as special \(Spin(7)\) manifold. However, the problem is the obstruction theory has more than two terms and methods of [BF1997] can not be applied. With a lot of work in derived algebraic geometry, including [Lurie2004a, TV2006, PTV+2013, BBJ2019], the Donaldson-Thomas type invariants in CY 4-folds were established in [CL2015] (special case) and [BJ2017] (general case) using analytic methods. More recently, algebraic virtual class is obtained in [OT2021]. Many results for CY3 can be transported to CY4 case, including some cases of MNOP conjectures (also in [CL2015]), the dimension zero DT generating seris in [CK2018]. the DT/PT correspondence in [CK2020], the relation to Gopakumar-Vafa invariants in [CMT2021, CMT2018, COT2022, COT2022a] and maybe a lot more. As the Donaldson-Thomas type invariants are defined for surfaces by Mochizuki in [Mochizuki2009], it is then a natural question, whether Donaldson-Thomas invariants can be generalized to even higher dimensions, since we are not using special holonomy in construction. For some work in this direction, the counting of dimension zero subschemes is studied in [CK2019]. As a reminder, Gromov-Witten theory is well defined for varieties of arbitrary dimension.

MNOP conjectures

[2021-12-31 Fri 14:27] The MNOP conjectures are proposed in [MNOP2006] (primary case), [MNOP2006a] (descent case). Some basic examples such as toric local surface were considered there. MNOP conjectures are a series of conjectures states some kind of equivalence between the generating series of Gromov-Witten invariants and Donaldson-Thomas invariants. There are two potential motivation behind the conjecture: first, these two invarinats both counting “curves”; second, the correspondence might be the analog of Gromov-Witten/Seiberg-Witten correspondence in dimesion four established by Taubes as an mathematical gauge/string duality. The methods in [MNOP2006] and [MNOP2006a] are based on topological vertex introduced in [AKM+2005] the 1-leg case established in [LLZ2003], and 2-leg case established in [LLZ2006] and virtual localization technique in [GP1999].

The first conjectures about the zero dimensional Donaldson-Thomas generating series was proved independently in [Li2006, BF2008, LP2009], using quite different methods.

A big step in MNOP conjectures is the introduction of Pandharipande-Thomas (PT) invariants, which is introduced in [PT2009]. By replacing the moduli space of ideal sheaves by moduli space of stable pairs, the generating series becomes easier to handle. It gives a geometric meaning to the reduced Donaldson-Thomas generating series. The two authors conjectured the correspondence between DT and PT, which was proved for Calabi-Yau varieties in [Toda2010] and [Bridgeland2011]. The Toda’s proof is based on some weak stability condition and Bridgelands proof uses motivic Hall algebra introduced mainly by Joyce. It is worth noting that the DT/PT correspondence can be viewed as a special case of wall-crossing. One corollary of PT/DT correspondence is the rationality part of the MNOP conjectures.

The GW/DT correspondence for arbitrary toric varieties in primary case was proved in toric case [MOOP2011] and therefore gives the proof of the formula of topological vertex in 3-leg case. The idea is based on degeneration formula of Gromov-Witten and Donaldson-Thomas invariants introduced in [IP2003, Li2002, LW2015, Wu2007a] to reduce the problem to some local models where the correspondnece has been established in local curves case [OP2010, BP2008, OP2010a], and \(A^{n} \times \mathbb{P}^{1}\) case [Maulik2009, MO2009, MO2009a]. As another application, based on GW/PT correspondence, in toric variety case, Virasoro constraint for stable pairs was first obtained in and therefore for GW theory is obtained in [MOO+2020].

The establishment of GW/PT correspondence seems to be easier than GW/DT correspondence. GW/PT correspondence in toric variety case with descent insertion was given in [PP2014] and the local complete intersection in product of projective space case was established latter in [PP2017]. The authors first consider the descent theory for PT invariants in [PP2013, PP2014], and then established the correpondence in more local models (for example \(\mathbb{P}^{1}\) bundles over K3 surfaces) and degenerate the complete intersection case to these local models via the dengeration scheme in [MP2006]. The explicit formula of the matrix coefficient for descent correspondence is given in [OOP2020].

Despite these advances, as far as I know, the most general form of the conjecture is still open, both for Calabi-Yau and arbitrary 3-folds. The main difficulties in my opinion is the restricted power of degeneration methods for general varieties. However, the study of the moduli space underlying these enumerative problem seems to be even more formidable.

Homological mirror symmetry

[2021-12-31 Fri 14:46] If Langlands program is hydra-like conjectures in algebra, then mirror symmetry is a hydra-like conjectures in geometry. After some efforts pursuing Hodge-theoretic mirror symmetry, homological mirror symmetry was proposed by Kontsevich in [Kontsevich1995], which is one of the most profound conjecture in modern geometry, still under active research. Roughly speaking, it suggests some deep similarities between symplectic geometry and complex algebraic geometry of Calabi-Yau manifolds. More specifically, it states derived category of some variant of Fukaya category on \(X\) (called A-side) is equivalent to derived category of coherent sheaves of its mirror partner \(X^{\vee}\) (called B-side), and derived category of Fukaya category of \(X^{\vee}\) is equivalent to derived category of coherent sheaves on \(X\).

The physics of mirror symmetry is 2d \(\mathcal{N} = 2\) SCFT, and homological mirror symmetry is one way to describe this duality in mathematics. Another famous conjecture is SYZ conjecture. The homological mirror symmetry is related to D-branes, where ‘derived’ category then arises (For example, see [Aspinwall2009]).

Like the case of MNOP conjecture, the homological mirror symmetry has been (partially) proved in many cases. Though the case related to physics is Calabi-Yau 3-fold, the conjecture was first checked in the most simple CY variety, elliptic curve, in [PZ1998]. There are also many other known cases, for example, abelian variety in [Fukaya2002], quartic surfaces in [Seidel2015] (the arxiv version appears about 2003), also for CY hypersurfaces in [Sheridan2015] and recently for CY intersection in toric varieties in [SS2021]. I have to emphasize that in most cases, only one direction of correspondence is proved, that is only the equivalence between derived category of Fukaya category of \(X\) and the derived category of coherent sheaves on \(X^{\vee}\) is proved and other side is omitted.

Soon after the proposal of the homological mirror symmetry for Calabi-Yau varieties, Kontesvich suggests another categorical mirror symmetry for Fano varieties in [Kontsevich1998]. The mirror of a Fano variety \(X\) is a Laudau-Ginzburg model, that is a pair \((X^{\vee}, W)\), where \(W: X \to \mathbb{C}\) is called potential. The A-side of Laudau-Ginzburg model is Fukaya-Seidel category [Seidel2001a, Seidel2001] which is conjecturally equivalent to derived category of coherent sheaves of \(X\). The B-side of Laudau-Ginzburg model is category of singularities [Orlov2004] and is conjecturally equivalent to Fukaya category of \(X\). There are also many (partial) proofs for Fano cases, for example \(\PP^{2}\) case in [Seidel2001], del Pezzo surfaces in [AKO2006], weighted projective planes in [AKO2008], and Fano hypersurfaces in [Sheridan2016].

A more recent proposal generalize the homological mirror symmetry to manifolds of general type [KKO+2009]. The mirror of a manifold of general type is also a Laudau-Ginzburg model. The genus 2 curve case was established in [Seidel2011] and higher genus case in [Efimov2012]. The methods also extended to pairs of pants and their higher dimensional analogs in [Sheridan2011] and punctured spheres in [AAE+2013]. In non-compact cases, Fukaya category is replaced by wrapped Fukaya categories.

Another interesting homological mirror symmetry is for toric varieties. Based on work of [NZ2009, FLT+2011], a variant version of homological mirror symmetry for toric varieties in equivariant case was proved in [FLT+2012]. More recently, the non-equivariant case was proved in [Kuwagaki2020]. This version of homological mirror symmetry is a little different form others by requiring some additional support conditions. However, it also gives some duality between symplectic geometry and complex geometry.

Also similar to the case of MNOP, the most general case, as far as I know, is wide open. Even the definition of Fukaya category for general symplectic manifolds is still missing, preventing any mathematically rigorous conjecture in these cases. Also, finding mirror partner for a given Calabi-Yau variety seems to be a challenging problem. Most methods are suggested by physicists (for example, [BB1997]) and is restricted and hard to generalize.

Derived category of coherent sheaves and Bridgeland stability

As Donaldson-Thomas theory counts (semi)stable sheaves, the notation of stability is important in the theory. [JS2012] study the wall crossing phenomenon when changing stability condition for abelian category of coherent sheaves. A more ambitious proposal by [KS2008] replace the abelian category by triangulated category, and Gieseker or \(\mu\)-stability by Bridgeland stability introduced in [Bridgeland2007]. The derived category of coherent sheaves can be replaced by Calabi-Yau categories, allowing fruitful interactions with many algebraic problems, including representation of quivers in [DM2020] and cluster algebras in [Davison2018]. More recently, Bridgeland suggesting a construction of \(\tau\) functions, a series of important functions in mathematical physics, from Donaldson-Thomas theory of Calabi-Yau categories in [Bridgeland2020a, Bridgeland2020, Bridgeland2019], showing their deep connection to mathematical physics.

The study of Bridgeland stability is also active. The construction of Bridgeland stability condition on derived category of coherent sheaves is a very challenging problem. The elliptic curve case is studied in [Bridgeland2006] and K3 surface in [Bridgeland2008] by tilting the heart. In [BMT2014], authors suggested a double-tilting methods to construct Bridgeland stability condition on 3-folds based on generalized Bogomolov-Gieseker inequality. Though the latter inequality was wrong [Schmidt2017], the idea is used to successfully construct the stability condition for 3-folds in various cases, including abelian threefolds in [BMS2016], some Fano threefolds in [Li2018], quintic threefold in [Li2019]. A remarkable property of Bridgeland stability is that the stability space is local homeomorphic to the complex linear space generated by numerical Grothendieck group of the category, and therefore admits a complex manifold structure. The study of this space has applications to moduli of sheaves [BM2014a], autoequivalence of derived category of coherent sheaves [BB2017], Donaldson-Thomas invariants [Toda2013] and Reid-type theorem [BBM+2014]. We can also construct the moduli space of Bridgeland (semi)stable objects [BM2014], which shares similar properties of moduli space of Gieseker (semi)stable objects. More recently, in [BLM+2021] the stability in families is studied to prove the deformation invariance of Donaldson-Thomas theory of Bridgeland stable objects in Calabi-Yau threefolds and propose a new method for constructing stability condition on threefolds.

Gauge theory and special holonomy

This part is still under construction due to my ignorance on this subject.

The gauge theory, including Donaldson theory, Seiberg-Witten theory and Floer theory, gains fruitful result in low dimensional topology, including astonishing Donaldson diagonalizability theorem and construction of exotic structure on \(\mathbb{R}^{4}\). The gauge theoretic methods are also very useful in speical holonomy. As Donaldson-Thomas invariants are analog of Casson invariants for Calabi-Yau 3-folds [DT1998], it may be useful to understand the Donaldson-Thomas theory from a gauge theoretic viewpoint. The Seiberg-Witten/Gromov-Witten correspondence established in a series of papers [Taubes2000] as an example of gauge/string duality may be viewed as a low dimension analog of GW/DT correspondence.
